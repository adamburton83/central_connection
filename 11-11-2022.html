November 11, 2022


On November 11, 1919, President Wilson declared on Armistice Day,

"To us in America, the reflections of Armistice Day will be filled with solemn pride in the heroism of those who died in the country’s service and with gratitude for the victory, both because of the thing from which it has freed us and because of the opportunity it has given America to show her sympathy with peace and justice in the councils of the nations…"
We now celebrate Veteran's Day on November 11. We live in the greatest country on earth, and that is because of the dedication of countless men and women who not only swore an oath to protect the Constitution of the United States but sacrificed greatly for the safety of all citizens. To all the veterans, thank you.

As I was writing last week's Central Connection, I was looking forward to church on Sunday. I guess God had different plans. I came down with flu symptoms on Saturday, which wiped me out for a few days. I greatly missed you, and I look forward to returning to the pulpit this Sunday.

On the Southern Baptist Convention emphasis calendar, this week is Orphans and Widows Sunday. The Bible tells the church to take special care of our orphans and widows. James writes,

Pure and undefiled religion before God the Father is this: to look after orphans and widows in their distress and to keep oneself unstained from the world.
-James 1:27 CSB
Tomorrow, our ladies are hosting a Tea Party in honor of our widows. This event is a small way to let our widows know that we love them. I want to give a special thank you to Lori Stanley and Rachel Applegate for helping organize this event. Also, thank you to all the ladies coming to celebrate our widows. Let's pray that we all stay healthy and thank a veteran today.

Pray that we all stay healthy and thank a veteran today.

Faithfully,
Pastor Adam
